Exorlink

Let f be an ESOP expression over n variables. Then each product term in f consists of literals. Each of the n variables can occur in three different ways in a product term. Either it is contained as a positive literal, it is contained as a negative literal, or it is not contained at all. When comparing two product terms, the distance of these two terms is the number of variables that are different in both terms.

A pair of two product terms with distance d can be transformed into an ESOP expression with d terms using the Exorlink operation. One can think of the Exorlink operation as a chain of transformation, “adjusting” one different position at the time. Since there are d! different ways of choosing the order of positions, one can obtain d! different ESOP expressions using the Exorlink operation.
